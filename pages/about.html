How We Work
📋

Our Mission

The Federal Times exists to provide rigorous, fact-based reporting on the institutions, policies, and events that shape American life. We believe an informed citizenry is essential to a functioning democracy, and our journalism is guided by that conviction above all else. Our coverage prioritizes public interest over commercial interest, and our editorial decisions are made independently of advertisers, investors, or political affiliations.

✅

Fact-Checking Process

Every factual claim published on The Federal Times goes through a three-stage verification process. Primary sources are identified and contacted wherever possible. Claims from secondary sources are independently corroborated by at least two additional sources. Our dedicated fact-checking desk reviews all major stories before publication, and corrections are published prominently and transparently when errors are identified.

🤖

AI + Human Hybrid Journalism

We leverage artificial intelligence as a tool to enhance — not replace — human journalism. Our AI systems assist with data aggregation, pattern recognition in large datasets, and initial research synthesis. Every piece of content published under a byline is written, reported, and edited by credentialed human journalists. AI is disclosed whenever it contributes meaningfully to the research or production of a story.

⚖️

Editorial Independence

The Federal Times maintains complete separation between its editorial and commercial operations. No advertiser, partner, or investor has any input into our editorial decisions. Our journalists do not accept gifts, travel, or other benefits from sources, corporations, or government entities. We do not participate in "pay to play" journalism, native advertising disguised as editorial, or any form of sponsored coverage without explicit disclosure.

🔍

Transparency & Corrections

We hold ourselves to the same scrutiny we apply to the institutions we cover. Corrections are published at the top of affected articles, not buried at the bottom. Our editorial standards document is publicly available. When we make a significant reporting error, we explain what went wrong and how our processes are being updated to prevent recurrence. We do not silently edit published content.

🛡️

Native Advertising Policy

The Federal Times participates in native advertising networks including Outbrain. All sponsored content, recommended content, and native advertisements are clearly labeled and visually distinguished from editorial content. Our journalists have no involvement in the production of sponsored content. Revenue from advertising does not influence which stories are covered or how they are framed.
